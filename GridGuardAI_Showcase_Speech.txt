South Africa loses R23 billion every year to electricity theft. Illegal “tap-offs” don’t just drain municipal revenue; they overload transformers, causing explosions that cost between R200,000 and R500,000 each to replace. Current metering can’t see theft that bypasses the meter entirely, and sending crews into high-theft zones puts technicians at risk. This is the gap GridGuard AI closes.

GridGuard AI is the scalpel your grid deserves. Instead of blunt shutdowns that punish entire neighbourhoods, we detect and surgically isolate only the illegal line — in under 300 milliseconds.

How it works:
1) Energy Balancing at the pole: we continuously compute I_pole − Σ I_meters and flag anomalies in real time.
2) AI Validation: a CNN–LSTM model on Huawei ModelArts analyzes vibration and electrical signatures to confirm theft with 93–99% confidence.
3) Scalpel Isolation: via Huawei IoTDA, a targeted MQTT command disconnects only the unauthorized bypass. Paying customers stay powered.
4) Live Operations: the Next.js 14 dashboard updates instantly using SignalR from our .NET 9 backend, streaming telemetry and alerts to field teams and control rooms.

Architecture:
• Backend: .NET 9 orchestrates telemetry, AI calls, and control flows
• Database: TimescaleDB stores time-series grid telemetry
• IoT: Huawei IoTDA provides secure MQTT messaging to relays
• AI: Huawei ModelArts runs the theft-validation pipeline
• Frontend: Next.js 14 delivers a real-time operational dashboard

Why it matters:
• Revenue Protection: detecting bypasses restores billable energy at the node level
• Safety: targeted isolation prevents transformer overloads and dangerous faults
• Precision: sub-second response minimizes customer impact and service calls
• Accountability: forensic traces and audit reports strengthen prosecution and deterrence
